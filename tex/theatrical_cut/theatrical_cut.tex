\section{Introduction}
Let $f\left( x \right) = x^n + ax + b$ for some $a,b \in \Z$ and $n \in \N$.
If $ab = 0$ we can calculate the Galois group over $\Q$ of each factor of $f$ using Kummer's theory.
When $ab \neq 0$, most existing literature handles the case of an irreducible $f$, and focuses on finding sufficient conditions for its Galois group to be $S_n$.
% Uchida \cite{Uchida} and Yamamoto \cite{Yamamoto} proved that if $f$ is irreducible, $n$ is prime and $1 = \gcd\left( an - a, bn \right)$, then the Galois group of $f$ is $S_n$.
Perhaps the most important result of this kind is that of Osada \cite{Osada1987TheGG} which treated the largegr family of polynomials of the form $x^n + ax^l + b$.
In our setup and for sufficiently large $n$, his result is equivalent to the statement that if $f$ is irreducible and $1 = \gcd\left( an - a, bn \right)$, then the Galois group of $f$ is $S_n$.
In particular Osaded deduces that the Galois group of $x^n - x - 1$ is $S_n$ for all $n$.
His work expanded upon the work of Uchida \cite{Uchida} and Yamamoto \cite{Yamamoto} and was later expanded upon by Cohen \cite{COHEN1999561} and Ao, Fei and Hong \cite{AoFeiHong2021}.
Fixing $a$ and $b$, all of their results, except the mentioned example of $x^n - x - 1$, require some congruence conditions on $n$.
