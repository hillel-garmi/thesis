\title{
  The Galois group of factors of $x^n + ax + b$ for small values of $a,b \in \Z$.
}
\author{Hilel Garmi}
\maketitle
By Hilbert's irreducibility theorem \cite{Hilbert}, almost any rational polynomial of degree $n$ has Galois group $S_n$ over $\Q$.
Nevertheless, parametrizing an example of such a polynomial for each $n$ remained a difficult problem.
Only 38 years after Hilbert's work, Schur \cite{schur1931affectless} showed that the following family of generalized Laguerre polynomials 
\[
  L_n^{\left( 0 \right)} := \sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k} \frac{\left( -x \right)^k}{k!}
\]
is such a parametrized example.
Later, the same was proven for other families of generalized Laguerre polynomials, such as the Hermite polynomials, $L^{\left( \pm 1/2 \right)}_n$ \cite{schur1931affectless}, and the reverse Bessel polynomials, $L^{\left( -2n-1 \right)}_n$ \cite{grosswald1978bessel} \cite{filaseta2002irreducibility}.
However, the only known example with a bounded height, is the family Selmer polynomials $f_n\left( x \right) = x^n - x - 1$  which was shown to be irreducible by Selmer \cite{SelmerIrreducibility1956} and to have Galois group $S_n$ by Nart and Vila \cite{NartVila1979} 
\editorial{Right? Neither I nor Gemini could find any other example}.
To achieve that, they first showed that all the inertia groups of $f_n$ are either trivial or generated by a transposition.
They then utilized Minkowski's bound to prove that the Galois group of $f_n$ over $\Q$ is generated by its inertia groups, and deduced that it is $S_n$ by some well known facts about permutation groups.
It thus makes sense to try to apply a similar strategy for $f_n\left( x \right) = x^n + ax + b$, or its irreducible factors, for some other values of $a,b \in \Z$.

Nart and Vila \cite{NartVila1979} themselves and Osada \cite{Osada1987TheGG} after them indeed generalized the above method to obtain some sufficient criteria on $a,b$ and $n$ for the Galois group of $f_n$ to be $S_n$, assuming its irreducibility.
Yet, each of these criteria includes some congruence condition on $n$, leaving out a positive density of the natural numbers as exceptions.
Maybe the most istructive example for how these congruence conditions arise is the actually reducible case of $f_n = x^n - 2x + 1$, which prompted our own interest in the subject through a recent result concerning it due to Bary-Soroker, Shusterman, and Zannier \cite[Appendix A]{Soroker_appendix_2017}.
In this case, $f_n$ factors as the product of $x-1$ and the polynomial $g\left( x \right) = x^{n-1} + x^{n-2} + \cdots + x - 1$.
The latter polynomial was shown to be irreducible by Miles \cite{Miles1960} and Wolfram \cite{Wolfram1998}, which were in fact interested in its close relative, the generalized Fibonacci polynomial $x^{n-1} - x^{n-2} - \cdots - x - 1$.
Martin \cite{MARTIN2004213} succeded, using methods similar to those used in the classical case of $x^n - x - 1$, to show that the Galois group of $g$ is $S_{n-1}$ for any odd value of $n$.
The reason his proof fails for even values of $n$, is that in this case 
\[
  x^n - 2x + 1 \equiv x^n - 1 \equiv \left( x^{n/2} - 1\right)^2 \mod 2
\]
and thus the inertia group of $f_n$ over $\Q_2$ is not generated by a transposition anymore.

In this lecture I will present a proof that bypasses this problem.
The previous proofs, both for the cases of generalized Laguerre polynomials, and for the case of $x^n - x - 1$ and its partial generalizations, focused on local methods, which in the generalizations of the case of $x^n - x - 1$ gave rise to the mentioned congruence conditions on $n$. 
Our new proof focuses on global bounds on the discriminant of number fields, like the mentioned Minkowski's bound and the stronger Odlyzko's bound \cite{OdlyzkoBoundsII}, to show that the "unproblematic" inertia groups, those that are indeed generated by transpositions, already generate the entire Galois group of $f_n$, rendering the problematic ones dispensable.
Using this approach, for some small values of $a,b \in \Z$, and in particular in any case where $a,b \in \left\{ \pm 1, \pm 2 \right\}$, and for any $n \in \N$, we were able to determine the factorization of $f_n$ and show that the Galois group of each of its irreducible factor, call it $g$, is indeed $S_{\deg g}$.
