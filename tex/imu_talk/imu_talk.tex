\title{
  The Galois group of factors of $x^n + ax + b$ for small values of $a,b \in \Z$.
}
\author{Hilel Garmi}
\maketitle
Let $f\left( x \right) = x^n + ax + b$ for some $a,b \in \Z$ and $n \in \N$.
If $ab = 0$ we can calculate the Galois group over $\Q$ of each factor of $f$ using Kummer's theory.
When $ab \neq 0$, most existing literature handles the case of an irreducible $f$, and focuses on finding sufficient conditions for its Galois group to be $S_n$.
For example, for all $n \in \N$, Selmer \cite{SelmerIrreducibility1956} showed that $x^n - x - 1$ is irreducible, and Nart and Vila \cite{NartVila1979} showed that its Galois group is $S_n$.
Uchida \cite{Uchida} and Yamamoto \cite{Yamamoto} showed already beforehand that if $f$ is irreducible and $n$ is prime, then the Galois group of $f$ is $S_n$.
Nart and Vila \cite{NartVila1979} and Osada \cite{Osada1987TheGG} found some more general criteria.
%  and Cohen Movahhedi and Salinier (\cite{Cohen1997} and \cite{COHEN1999}) found even more general criteria that ensure primitivity of the Galois group.
However, fixing $a$ and $b$, except in the mentioned example of $x^n - x - 1$, each of these criteria includes some congruence condition on $n$, leaving out a positive density of the natural numbers.
We show, that for some small values of $a,b \in \Z$, and in particular in any case where $a,b \in \left\{ \pm 1, \pm 2 \right\}$, the Galois group over $\Q$ of any factor of $x^n + ax + b$ is the full symmetric group for all $n \in \N$.

One reducable case that has been studied is the case $f\left( x \right) = x^n - 2x + 1$.
In this case, $f$ is a product of $x-1$ and the polynomial 
\[
  g\left( x \right) = x^{n-1} + x^{n-2} + \cdots + x - 1
\]
The roots of $g$ are the reciprocals of the roots of the generalized Fibonacci recurrence polynomial
\[
  h\left( x \right) = x^{n-1} - x^{n-2} - \cdots - x - 1
\]
which was shown by Miles \cite{Miles1960} and Wolfram \cite{Wolfram1998} to be irreducible.
Therefore, $g$ is also irreducible and has the same Galois group as $h$.
For any odd value of $n$, the Galois group of $h$ was shown to be $S_{n-1}$ by Martin \cite{MARTIN2004213}.
Soroker, Shusterman and Zannier \cite[Appendix A]{Soroker_appendix_2017} proved that for any $n$, the field defined over $\Q$ by $h$ has no totally real subextension.
In fact, to prove that the Galois group of $g$ is $S_{n-1}$, we extend their result and showed that the field defined by $h$ has no nontrivial subextensions at all.

We will present the strategy of the proof that the Galois group is $S_n$ in the cases where $f$ is irreducible.
The strategy for the reducible cases is similar.
We first show the key fact, that for any prime number $p$ not dividing $ab$, and any embedding of $\overline{\Q}$ into $\overline{\Q_p}$, the image of the absolute inertia group of $\Q_p$ in the Galois group of $f$ is either trivial or generated by a transposition.
From that key fact we were able to deduce that if the Galois group of $f$ is not $S_n$, then there is some nontrivial subextension $\Q \subsetneq K \subseteq \Q\left[ x \right] / \left( f \right)$ that is ramified only above primes that divide $ab$.
This deduction is done by combining some well known facts about permutation groups, together with the famous correspondence between number fields and finite sets with a transitive action of the absolute Galois group of $\Q$.
The next ingredient in the proof is comprised of bounding the root discriminant of $K$, by bounding its $p$-adic valuation for each $p$ dividing $ab$.
In cases where the bound is small enough, there are only very few number fields with root discriminant below that bound, that are ramified only above the primes that divide $ab$, and we can rule out by hand the possibility that each of these fields is contained in $\Q\left[ x \right] / \left( f \right)$.
